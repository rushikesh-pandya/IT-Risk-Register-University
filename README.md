## 📋 Project Overview

A complete GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance) project built from scratch as part of my MSc in Enterprise & IT Security. It simulates a real-world IT risk assessment for a fictional University IT Department — identifying, scoring, and proposing treatment for 13 IT risks across cybersecurity, operations, compliance, and third-party/physical domains.

## 🎯 What This Project Covers

- **13 risks identified** across 4 domains: Cybersecurity, Operational, Compliance, Third-party & Physical
- **Qualitative scoring model** — Likelihood × Impact (1–5 scale), max score 25
- **For every risk:** threat source, affected asset, current controls, treatment strategy (Mitigate / Accept / Transfer), recommended action, and a named risk owner
- **1 Critical risk** — Phishing (score: 20/25)
- **8 High risks** — including ransomware, unauthorised access to student records, IT system downtime, inadequate backup & recovery, GDPR non-compliance, cloud provider outage, and physical theft of IT equipment
- **4 Medium risks** — including research data compromise, key-person risk, cybersecurity audit gaps, software licensing, and open Wi-Fi exploitation

## 📐 Methodology
 
Each risk is scored using a standard 5×5 qualitative matrix:
 
> **Risk Score = Likelihood (1–5) × Impact (1–5)**
 
| Band | Score range |
|------|-------------|
| ⬜ Low | 1–5 |
| 🟢 Medium | 6–10 |
| 🟡 High | 11–19 |
| 🔴 Critical | 20–25 |
 
The heat map plots each risk by Likelihood (vertical axis) against Impact (horizontal axis), with every cell shaded by the same Likelihood × Impact bands — so the visual matches the register exactly.
